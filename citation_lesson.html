üìö What Are In-Text Citations?

In-text citations are brief references within your paper that point readers to the full source information in your bibliography or works cited page. They're like road signs that guide your readers to your sources!

üìñ APA Style Citations

APA (American Psychological Association) style is commonly used in social sciences, education, and psychology.

Basic Format: (Author, Year)
Example: Climate change affects global temperatures (Smith, 2023).
With Page Number: (Author, Year, p. #)
Example: "Global warming is accelerating" (Johnson, 2022, p. 45).
Multiple Authors:
Two authors: (Smith & Jones, 2023)
Three or more: (Brown et al., 2023)

üìú MLA Style Citations

MLA (Modern Language Association) style is typically used in literature, arts, and humanities.

Basic Format: (Author Page#)
Example: Shakespeare explores themes of love and betrayal (Wilson 234).
With Quote:
Example: The author states that "literature reflects society" (Davis 67).
No Author: ("Title" Page#)
Example: Recent studies show improvement ("Education Today" 12).

üèõÔ∏è Chicago Style Citations

Chicago style offers two systems: Notes-Bibliography and Author-Date. Here's the Author-Date system:

Basic Format: (Author Year, Page#)
Example: Historical evidence supports this claim (Thompson 2023, 89).
Multiple Works:
Example: Several studies confirm this (Adams 2022; Baker 2023; Clark 2024).
